Rapid Sequence Intubation

Description

RSI or Rapid Sequence Intubation is a process commonly used in the emergency room setting for patients who cannot maintain their airway. The steps of the ingredients in this recipes are extremely important but the ingredients can vary. Don't worry though! There is a way to remember the order, passed on throughout generations of Emergency Medicine staff in the form of a cheeky sentence: "Don't succ before you date.". This uses the names of two of the most common RSI medications succinylcholine and etomidate to remind you to not use a paralytic before a sedative.

Ingredients

  • A paralytic(most commonly rocuronium or succinylcholine)
  • A sedative(etomidate, propofol or ketamine are common choices)
  • A provider ready to intubate
